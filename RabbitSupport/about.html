About Us

Paradise Rabbit Support is situation in the small village of in essex. The centre was started 10 years ago by local resident Mary Charmers in the back garden of her house with just a few pens for the Rabbits to live in.

The Centre is still in the back garden but now comprises of 3 separate housing blocks and two large runs all of which have been financed and built with the help of the volunteers who help to run the Centre and look after the Rabbits.

The aim of the Centre is to give a chance for unwanted and abandoned Rabbits to find a new life with new owners.

The Centre holds open days and various other events to raise money to support their aims. We take in homeless rabbits every day. Even if you can't volunteer with us at the rabbit center, please consider helping out at a shelter near you.

Here are some ways you can help shelter bunnies:

  • bring them fresh vegetables;
  • groom them;
  • play with them;
  • socialize with them;
  • take their pictures and write bios for them;
  • take them to adoption events;
  • coordinate spays and neuters;
  • educate staff, volunteers, and potential adopters about rabbits and rabbit care;
  • foster one or two of them when the shelter gets too full.
